  You've identified a major layout problem! Here's what's happening:

  The Conflict

  <main className="... flex gap-6 lg:gap-8 lg:pl-10">
    <div className="w-full xl:w-3xl mx-auto">  ← Problem here!
      <Outlet />
    </div>
    <aside className="w-64">
      <TableOfContents />
    </aside>
  </main>

  Layout Analysis

  At xl breakpoint (≥1280px):

  <main> with flex container and lg:pl-10
  ├─ <div> with xl:w-3xl mx-auto  ← Constrained to ~48rem + auto margins
  └─ <aside> with w-64             ← Fixed 16rem width

  The Issues:

  1. mx-auto Centers the Content: The content div will try to center itself with
   auto margins, pushing against both the left padding (lg:pl-10) and the TOC
  aside.
  2. Width Constraint Breaks Flex Flow: xl:w-3xl (likely ~48rem) constrains the
  flex child, which conflicts with the gap-6 lg:gap-8 trying to distribute space
   between content and TOC.
  3. Asymmetric Layout: You have:
    - Left: lg:pl-10 (2.5rem) padding
    - Center: Content with auto margins trying to center
    - Right: gap-8 (2rem) + TOC (16rem)

  This creates visual imbalance.

  Visual Problem

  Desktop XL (≥1280px):
  [Sidebar] ←pl-10→ [??Auto margin?? Content (3xl width) ??Auto margin??]
  ←gap-8→ [TOC 16rem]
                     ↑ This will look weird and off-center

  Recommendations

  Option 1: Remove mx-auto (Recommended)

  <main className="... flex gap-6 lg:gap-8 justify-between lg:pl-10">
    <div className="w-full min-w-0 xl:w-3xl">  {/* Remove mx-auto */}
      <Outlet />
    </div>
    <aside className="w-64">
      <TableOfContents />
    </aside>
  </main>

  Why? The justify-between on <main> already handles horizontal distribution.
  The mx-auto creates centering conflict.

  Option 2: Use Container Gap Instead

  <div className="flex container mx-auto lg:gap-10">  {/* Gap on container */}
    <Sidebar ... />
    <main className="... flex gap-6 lg:gap-8 justify-between">  {/* Remove
  lg:pl-10 */}
      <div className="w-full min-w-0 xl:w-3xl mx-auto">

  Why? Moves spacing responsibility to the parent, keeps content centering
  intact.

  Option 3: Symmetric Padding Instead of Margin

  <main className="... flex gap-6 lg:gap-8 justify-between lg:px-10">  {/* px 
  instead of pl */}
    <div className="w-full min-w-0 xl:max-w-3xl">  {/* max-w instead of w */}

  Why? Symmetric padding + max-width is clearer intent than fixed width + auto
  margins in flex.

  My Recommendation

  Go with Option 1 — remove mx-auto and keep your lg:pl-10 approach:

  <main className="w-full min-w-0 flex py-6 md:py-10 gap-6 lg:gap-8 
  justify-between max-lg:flex-wrap lg:pl-10">
    <div className="w-full min-w-0 xl:w-3xl">
      <Outlet />
    </div>
    <aside className="w-64">
      <TableOfContents />
    </aside>
  </main>

  The justify-between handles distribution, xl:w-3xl constrains content width,
  and TOC stays on the right. No centering conflicts.

  Great catch on spotting this potential layout issue!

  You've identified a major layout problem! Here's what's happening:

  The Conflict

  <main className="... flex gap-6 lg:gap-8 lg:pl-10">
    <div className="w-full xl:w-3xl mx-auto">  ← Problem here!
      <Outlet />
    </div>

      <TableOfContents />
    </aside>

  Layout Analysis

  At xl breakpoint (≥1280px):

  <main> with flex container and lg:pl-10
  ├─ <div> with xl:w-3xl mx-auto  ← Constrained to ~48rem + auto margins
  └─ <aside> with w-64             ← Fixed 16rem width

  The Issues:

  1. mx-auto Centers the Content: The content div will try to center itself with
   auto margins, pushing against both the left padding (lg:pl-10) and the TOC
  aside.
  2. Width Constraint Breaks Flex Flow: xl:w-3xl (likely ~48rem) constrains the
  flex child, which conflicts with the gap-6 lg:gap-8 trying to distribute space
   between content and TOC.
  3. Asymmetric Layout: You have:
    - Left: lg:pl-10 (2.5rem) padding
    - Center: Content with auto margins trying to center
    - Right: gap-8 (2rem) + TOC (16rem)

  This creates visual imbalance.

  Visual Problem

  Desktop XL (≥1280px):
  [Sidebar] ←pl-10→ [??Auto margin?? Content (3xl width) ??Auto margin??]
  ←gap-8→ [TOC 16rem]
                     ↑ This will look weird and off-center

  Recommendations

  Option 1: Remove mx-auto (Recommended)

  <main className="... flex gap-6 lg:gap-8 justify-between lg:pl-10">

    </div>
    <aside className="w-64">

    </aside>
  </main>

  Why? The justify-between on <main> already handles horizontal distribution.
  The mx-auto creates centering conflict.

  <div className="flex container mx-auto lg:gap-10">  {/* Gap on container */}
